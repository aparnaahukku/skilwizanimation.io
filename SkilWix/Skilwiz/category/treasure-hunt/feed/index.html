At Plastic Wax, we are unashamedly nerdy. I don’t know if it’s the fact that we build arcade machines in our board room or the armory of video game weaponry that adorns our studio walls that tips you off most. Either way, we’re huge fans of the pop culture work from US-based concept artist Zach Fischer.

Making a name for himself in the cosplay space for custom designing armoured, gender-swapped, anthropomorphised or just plain awesome costume designs of beloved characters, Fischer’s art puts a fresh, fantasy spin on some of our old favourites.

Head on over to Zach’s channels to see not only his inspiring designs, but the artists who bring them into reality through an impressive array of fabrication skills.

]]> Feel Good Friday from the Plastic Wax studio.

Happy Friday! We thought today would be the perfect day for a dose of inspiration from our treasure trove. Introducing Dallas Clayton – children’s author, artist and maybe the first human being to actually be composed of pure sunshine.
We’re inspired by Dallas for his unending sense of childlike wonder at the world. If you’re ever feeling down, we’d strongly suggest his instagram account for a reminder that butterflies exist, monsters can be adorable and just how long it’s been since you saw a set of crayons.

It doesn’t end at a heartwarming sketchpad – Clayton frequently takes his art outside the studio and into the real world, making it a little bit brighter every time. From his fully suited rainbow mascot The Encourager to painting an entire house from floor to ceiling, to creating murals with schoolkids about changing the world, his work is hard to define and we’re not sure we’d ever want to anyway.

We hope you’re as inspired as we are for your weekend!

]]> This installment of the Plastic Wax treasure hunt brings us back to world of Star Wars in a way we never expected.


From the troves of our browser bookmarks comes this stunning collection of concept art for the shelved “Star Wars 1313”. Gus Mendonca’s gallery of environments, weaponry and characters gives us a glimpse into the gritty, grimey underworld that lurks beneath the chromed skyscrapers of Coruscant we know so well.

The never released title was intended to follow the story of a young Boba Fett in search of a bounty for Jabba the Hutt. As cool as that sounds, what really interests us is the setting. An almost Bladerunner-esque take on the environments of the Star Wars universe is a mix that just works. That said, not sure how we feel about seeing a headless R2 unit…

This week at the Plastic Wax office, we’re a little in love with Loish.

A digital artist and animator, Loish’s work centers around gorgeous, vibrant characters in rich and often surreal environments.

About herself, Loish says, “I’ve been drawing since the day I could hold a pencil, and started teaching myself to draw digitally in 2003. Although I was born in Holland and have dutch nationality, I’ve lived all over the world, including the United States, Indonesia, France and Belgium.” Although it seems a little more like she lives on another, far more magical, planet.

Loish’s website is a treasure trove of close-up details of her paintings, world building sketches and progress imagery. You can check it out here, but be prepared to lose hours of your life.

]]> Here’s one for the concept artists! At Plastic Wax, we’re big believers in the talent of Australian artists. We’re constantly being inspired by the work of home grown creators, so in this Treasure Hunt we’re uncovering the work of Brisbane based concept artist Alex Allen.

Allen’s rough, painterly style manages to effortlessly capture a sense of cinematic movement and drama – often with gorgeous colour palettes and intriguing characters and settings. Above, he manages to totally embody the look and feel of Disney’s Maleficent with very little detail work.

In the office this week, we’re loving his detailed, sweeping landscapes just as much as his quick character sketches. Check out Alex Allen’s social channels for more of his work.
